Jakob Ludwig Felix Mendelssohn

Born : 3 February 1809 in Berlin (Frederic-Guillaume III)
Died : 1847

Father : Abraham Mendelssohn (1776-1835), banker
Mother : Lea Salomon (1777-1842), got a great education, musical by Kirnberger, student of Bach

Known ancestor :
- Moses Mendelssohn (1729-1786)
  Philosopher from the "Aufklärung"
  Writes a "Phaedon"
  Translates a lot of sacred text from hebrew to german especially the "Bi'ur"

Brother and sisters :
- Fanny Caecilie (1805-1847) -> the most important
- Rebecca (1811-1856)
- Paul Hermann (1813-1874)

Everyone is jew and got converted to christianism

Mendelssohn is a lutherian.

Famous friends :
- Ferdinand Hiller, met in 1822, Francfort
- Ferdinand David, Berlin
- Adolf Bernhard Marx, (the first one to talk about "sonata form" if I'm not mistaken)
- Ignaz MOscheles
- Johann Gustav Droysen, historicist
- Eduard Devrient, singer
- Eduard Rietz, violonist
- Carl Klingemann, poet
- Julius Schubring, theologist

Education :
- Parents
- G. A. H. Stenzel
- Carl Wilhelm Ludwig Heyse from his ten years

- get advice from Marie Bigot in 1816

- creation of "Undine" Hoffmann, 1816
              "Der Freischütz" Weber, 1821
              "Hans Heiling" Marschner, 1833

  conducted by Mendelssohn
- town of Meyerbeer

Pasionate about Shakespeare, thanks to Weber in 1821 and 1825.

Musical works :
- Piano and strings quartet in C minor op 1 (1822), für Anton Radziwill
- Piano and strings quartet in F minor op 2 (1823), für Zelter
- Piano and strings quartet in B minor op 3 (1825), für Goethe
among others chamber works not published -> a string quartet in Eb Major (1823)

- Violin and piano sonata in F minor op 4 (1825), für Rietz

(played the Hammerklavier sonata before Liszt in 1827 and transcribed the 9th symphony)
- Capriccio in F# minor op 5 (1825)
- Piano Sonata in E Major op 6 (1826)
(E minor/Major are important tonalities for Mendelssohn)

- 7 Charakterstücke op 7 (1824-1827), around E minor, für Berger
- Fantasia in E minor/Major op 15 (written in 1827, published in 1830), on "The last rose of summer"
- Fantasia in F# minor op 28 (begin in 1828 as the "Scottish sonata", published in 1833), für Moscheles
- Rondo Capriccioso op 14, (1828 with the add of a prelude in 1830),
  two part prelude then presto -> could be a romantic replacement to preludes and fugues

- Symphony in C minor op 11 (1824)

among pieces for solist instrument and orchestra
- Capriccio brillant in B minor op 22 (1825-1826)

Inspired by Beethoven op 130 Cavatine and op 132 "Heiliger Dankgesang eines Genesenen an die Gottheit in der lydischen Tonart"
- String quartet in Eb Major Op 12 (1829)
- String quartet in A minor Op 13 (1827-1832) cycle structure, from "Ist es wahr ?" op 9/1 (himself from "Muss es sein ?" op 135)
- String quintet in A Major Op 18 (1826)
- String Octet in E flat Major (1825)

- Overture "Ein Sommernachtstraum" op 21 (1826) ("A midnight summer dream" from Shakespeare)
- Ocerture "Meeresstille und glückligne Fahrt" op 27 (1828) from Goethe ("Calm sea and Prosperous Voyage")

Writes a lot of lieder (the first one on his father birthday)
- Lieder und Gesänge op 8 and 9 (1828 and 1830, 6 from these Opus are from Fanny)
- Some Liedspiel without opus number ("Ich, J Mendelssohn", "Die Soldatenliebschaft", "Die beiden Pädagogen",...)
- "Die Hochzeit des Camacho" op 10 (1827) - The Marriage of Camacho - Comic Opera in 2 actes
